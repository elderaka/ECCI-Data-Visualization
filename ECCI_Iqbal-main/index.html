Chapter 5 ¬∑ Honest Trade-offs

What If We Only Swap Engines?

Not every co-benefit is automatically positive. In the model, congestion, road safety, and hassle costs improve initially ‚Äì then deteriorate as electric vehicles become cheaper and total driving demand rebounds.

By the 2040s, these lines drop below zero: total time lost in traffic and the social cost of accidents outweigh baseline gains.

Key message: Electrification alone isn't enough. Cities need walkable design and strong public transport so net-zero feels like less hassle, not more.
